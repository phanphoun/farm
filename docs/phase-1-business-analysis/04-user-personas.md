# FarmJumnoy - Phase 1: User Personas

## Primary Personas

### 1. Sreyneath — "The Ambitious Young Farmer"
**Demographics**: 28, Female, Kampong Cham province, 1.2 ha rice + 0.3 ha vegetables
**Income**: $2,800/year (household), smartphone user (Samsung A-series, $150)
**Digital**: Facebook daily, YouTube for farming, TikTok evening, LINE family chat
**Literacy**: Completed Grade 12, reads Khmer fluently, basic English

**Goals**:
- Increase vegetable yield 30% to supply Phnom Penh market
- Get organic certification for premium price
- Learn drip irrigation & IPM (Integrated Pest Management)
- Build savings for 2nd motorbike (logistics)

**Pain Points**:
- Agro-dealer gives wrong pesticide advice (lost 40% crop last season)
- No direct market access — sells to middleman at 50% wholesale
- Can't afford $3,000 organic cert; no group cert option
- YouTube videos in English/Thai; Khmer content outdated

**Behaviors**:
- Watches 30-min Khmer farming videos at 1.5x speed
- Asks questions in 3 Facebook groups, waits hours for replies
- Buys inputs from local dealer (trusts him personally)
- Tracks expenses in paper notebook, loses receipts

**FarmJumnoy Value Prop**:
- Verified expert chat: "Ask~Srey" gets agronomist reply <30 min
- Khmer video course: "IPM for Yardlong Bean" with certificate
- Group certification via cooperative: $300/farmer
- Marketplace: Buy drip kit on installment (Wing pay-later)
- Farm dashboard: Digital records → credit score → microloan

**Success Metrics**: 25% yield increase Year 1; $500 additional income; completes 3 courses

### 2. Vuthy — "The Experienced Cooperative Leader"
**Demographics**: 45, Male, Battambang, 5 ha rice (coop manages 200 ha total)
**Income**: $8,500/year + coop dividends, smartphone + laptop
**Digital**: Facebook, Telegram (coop admin), email, Zoom for gov meetings
**Literacy**: University degree (Agriculture), fluent English, Khmer

**Goals**:
- Secure EUDR-compliant certification for 200 ha (export to EU)
- Digitize coop records for transparency & donor reporting
- Negotiate bulk input prices for 150 member farmers
- Train youth members in modern techniques

**Pain Points**:
- Paper-based traceability — audit nightmare
- Members resist new tech ("too complicated")
- Certification bodies charge $15K for coop audit
- No real-time visibility on member crop status
- Bank requires 3-year audited financials for loan

**Behaviors**:
- Manages coop via Telegram + Google Sheets + paper ledgers
- Attends MAFF training, shares printouts with members
- Personally visits 20 farms/week for monitoring
- Negotiates with exporters via email/Zoom

**FarmJumnoy Value Prop**:
- Coop dashboard: Real-time farm map, crop status, harvest forecast
- EUDR certification workflow: Digital traceability, inspector app, QR certs
- Bulk procurement: Pooled orders → 15% discount, vendor dashboard
- Training LMS: Assign courses, track completion, issue certificates
- Financial module: Auto-generate reports for bank/NGO donors

**Success Metrics**: EUDR cert obtained; 30% input cost reduction; 80% member digital adoption

### 3. Dr. Sopheak — "The Agronomy Expert"
**Demographics**: 38, Female, Phnom Penh (serves nationwide), PhD Plant Pathology
**Income**: $1,200/mo (gov salary) + $800/mo consulting, iPhone + MacBook
**Digital**: LinkedIn, ResearchGate, Zoom, Google Drive, Slack, Khmer/English

**Goals**:
- Scale advisory beyond 20 farmers/month capacity
- Monetize expertise: courses, consultations, certification inspecting
- Build reputation for international consulting
- Contribute to national pest surveillance

**Pain Points**:
- Repeat same advice to different farmers (no leverage)
- No trusted platform for paid consultations (FB informal, no payment)
- Course creation too technical (video editing, LMS)
- Certification inspection paper-based, slow payment
- Hard to prove impact for career advancement

**Behaviors**:
- Runs weekly Zoom clinic (free, 15 farmers max)
- Writes Khmer pest alerts for MAFF bulletin
- Reviews certification applications manually (PDF/email)
- Wants to create "Rice Disease Masterclass" but no time

**FarmJumnoy Value Prop**:
- Expert profile: Verified credentials, ratings, availability calendar
- AI-assisted responses: RAG from her papers + MAFF guidelines
- Course builder: Record once, sell forever, platform handles video/LMS/payment
- Inspector app: Digital checklists, photo evidence, auto-report, instant payout
- Impact dashboard: Farmers helped, yield increase attributed, income generated

**Success Metrics**: $2,000/mo platform income; 200 farmers advised/mo; 3 courses published

### 4. Sokha — "The Agri-Input Vendor"
**Demographics**: 42, Male, Siem Reap town, owns "Sokha Agri Supply" (3 staff)
**Income**: $3,500/mo revenue, 18% margin, Android phone + POS tablet
**Digital**: Facebook Business, Wing/TrueMoney merchant, basic Excel, Khmer

**Goals**:
- Expand beyond walk-in customers to provincial farmers
- Reduce inventory waste (expired pesticides = loss)
- Offer installment payments to win loyal farmers
- Become certified "Digital Champion" for gov programs

**Pain Points**:
- 60% sales cash-only, no customer data
- Farmers buy wrong product → blame vendor → reputation hit
- No online catalog; takes orders via Messenger (chaotic)
- Competitors undercut price; no differentiation
- Bank loan rejected — no digital transaction history

**Behaviors**:
- Posts product photos on FB daily, replies Messenger 8am-10pm
- Delivers by motorbike within 20km radius
- Gives verbal advice (learned from experience, not certified)
- Tracks stock in notebook, counts monthly

**FarmJumnoy Value Prop**:
- Vendor dashboard: Catalog, inventory, orders, analytics
- Verified vendor badge: Expert-approved products, farmer reviews
- Installment integration: Wing/TrueMoney pay-later, platform guarantees
- Demand forecast: AI predicts seasonal demand from farmer data
- Credit builder: Digital sales history → bank loan eligibility

**Success Metrics**: 40% online orders; 25% revenue growth; 15% inventory reduction; bank loan approved

### 5. Makara — "The NGO Program Manager"
**Demographics**: 35, Female, Phnom Penh, manages $2M/yr climate-smart ag program
**Income**: $2,500/mo, organization provides laptop, field visits monthly
**Digital**: Teams, PowerBI, KoBoToolbox, QGIS, English/Khmer fluent

**Goals**:
- Demonstrate impact to donors (yield, income, adoption metrics)
- Scale training from 500 to 5,000 farmers without 10x staff
- Collect verifiable data for carbon credit / EUDR reporting
- Coordinate with MAFF for policy alignment

**Pain Points**:
- Paper surveys → 3-month lag, 20% data loss, enumerator bias
- Training attendance ≠ adoption; no follow-up tracking
- Multiple partners use different tools (no data integration)
- Donor reports take 6 weeks to compile manually

**Behaviors**:
- Designs M&E framework, hires local enumerators
- Runs Training of Trainers (ToT) cascade model
- Reports to MAFF quarterly, donors semi-annually
- Uses KoBoToolbox for surveys, Excel for analysis

**FarmJumnoy Value Prop**:
- Partner dashboard: Real-time adoption, yield, income metrics
- Digital M&E: In-app surveys, auto-scoring, photo verification
- Training LMS integration: Assign, track, certify at scale
- Data API: Export to PowerBI/QGIS; carbon/MRV modules
- Gov alignment: MAFF-endorsed methodologies, shared indicators

**Success Metrics**: 80% data cost reduction; real-time donor reporting; 5,000 farmers onboarded
